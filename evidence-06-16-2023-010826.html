 		        It has come to our attention that the Borough Of Tenafly has a borough code enforcement division with employees Robert McQuade and Raishah Franklin (Removed from borough code enforcement officer position after several months on the job) that enforce something called the International Property Maintenance Code (IPMC) as well as other ordinances such as garage sale permits, etc. The IPMC ordinance appears quite convoluted and unable to be comprehended. Enforcement includes trespassing on private property within the borough and intruding on people of the community by knocking on doors and getting in the face of whoever answers even though they are not the owner of the property. Posting of flyers on doors of homes & buildings within Tenafly makes the property appear vacant, derelict, and/or abandoned and is an invitation to trespassers and vandals to peak their curiosity. Filing of orders through the mail. Initiation of legal proceedings with The Borough Of Tenafly municipal court for things such as uncut grass on your lawn or a pile of branches or twigs. We live in an area designated as tree city. So branches and twigs are more the norm than not. We've seen propertys with piles of branches left at the curb and not picked up by Tenafly for several monthe to a year. Residents shouldn't be called into Tenafly municipal court for Tenafly's negligence.

Step One may include a notice of "Overgrown Lawn".

Step Two may include an order to correct a condition say 300.1

Step Three may include a court summons and combined complaint in the form of a ticket that says "State Of New Jersey vs ______________________". The blank would be you the property owner or a resident of the lot. And the complaint would state Failure to Cut Grass.

Well, you the property owner were never told to cut the grass and the Borough has no right to tell you to cut the grass. Additionally ordinance 300.1 is not a state law nor is it the borough ordinance passed. Additionally for someone to file a complaint in a court of law they have to been harmed or damaged. In no way has a borough code enforcement officer experienced harm or damage.

Evidence from the community shows that they have spoken and made their experiences known regarding the aggressive nature of the Borough Of Tenafly code enforcement division. Evidence from the community shows that the ordinance is enforced on an arbitrary basis therefore violating constitutional rules of equality. And the ordinance appears to have been passed in an arbitrary and capricious manner. The ordinance in it's vastness and vagueness also appears unconstitutional. Evidence shows that Robert McQuade makes up his own rules of law regarding enforcement of the International Property Maintenance Code (IPMC) as well as other ordinances.

      This can be changed by petition to remove the said ordinance and removal of the borough code enforcement officer employment position. Plus it would save the borough thousands of dollars a year in salary expense and save hundreds reds of gallons of gas expenses, vehicle expenses and reduce gas pollution. The money saved could then be put to good use for resident needs such as maintaining roads. Also the borough budget will be lower and property owners taxes could be reduced.

      Said petition could take the form of a formal petition and voluntary removal by the Borough Of Tenafly or an ordinance by the council to remove said ordinance. Something they probably never thought of. Or the petition could be rolled into an initiative/referendum/ballot measure to be included at a municipal election.
